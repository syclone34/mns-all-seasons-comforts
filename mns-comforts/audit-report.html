🔍 Issue Findings
🔴 Critical
Issue #1: Broken Mobile Navigation Bar

When viewed on any mobile phone, the navigation menu does not collapse into a hamburger menu. Instead, nav links overflow off-screen and become completely inaccessible. Mobile visitors who land on the homepage cannot navigate to Services, About, or Contact — they are stuck on the homepage with no path forward. Since the majority of HVAC emergency searches happen on mobile devices, this issue is actively causing the business to lose booked service calls every day.

✅ RetroFit FixRebuild on a modern, responsive platform with a sticky hamburger navigation that smoothly reveals all links on tap for any screen size under 768px.

🔴 Critical
Issue #2: Inconsistent Local SEO Targeting

The page title and meta description target "Coon Rapids HVAC Repair," but the body content on every other page mentions "Maple Grove, Plymouth, and surrounding areas" with no reference to Coon Rapids. This geographic inconsistency sends contradictory signals to Google's local ranking algorithm. The site cannot rank strongly for any of the targeted cities because Google cannot determine where the business primarily operates. Local competitors with consistent geo-targeting will consistently outrank this site.

✅ RetroFit FixUnify messaging across every page. Create individual location landing pages for each target city (Coon Rapids, Maple Grove, Plymouth) with unique, keyword-optimized content for each market.
🟠 High
Issue #3: Duplicate Content Across Service Pages

The Residential and Commercial service pages are nearly identical — the same layout, the same paragraph structure, with only a few word swaps between "residential" and "commercial." Google identifies this as duplicate or thin content and suppresses one or both pages in search results, preventing either page from ranking for its target keywords.

✅ RetroFit FixRewrite each page with distinct, audience-specific content. Residential pages should address homeowner pain points (family comfort, energy bills, furnace breakdowns). Commercial pages should cover business uptime, maintenance contracts, and rooftop units.
🟠 High
Issue #4: No Customer Reviews or Social Proof

There are no testimonials, star ratings, or Google Reviews anywhere on the site. For local home service businesses, reviews are the #1 conversion factor. Homeowners deciding who to let into their home will look for proof that others have had a positive experience. Without social proof, the business appears less established than competitors prominently displaying their ratings. This directly reduces form submissions and call volume.

✅ RetroFit FixAdd a prominent testimonials section on the homepage with real customer names, locations, and star ratings. Integrate a live Google Business Profile review widget where possible.

🟡 Medium
Issue #5: Thin About Page — No Trust Building

The About page is a single short paragraph with no photos, no founder story, no certifications, and no credentials. For a home services business, the About page is one of the highest-converting pages — customers want to know who they are inviting into their home before they book. Competitors with personal stories, team photos, and certification badges (NATE, EPA 608) win more trust and more bookings.

✅ RetroFit FixRedesign the About page with the owner's story, team photos, years in business, certification badges, and local community ties to build personal trust.
🟡 Medium
Issue #6: No Online Scheduling or Booking

Service can only be requested by calling or submitting a basic contact form. A growing segment of homeowners — particularly under 45 — strongly prefer self-service online booking without making a phone call. Competitors offering online scheduling capture this segment entirely, especially for non-emergency maintenance appointments.

✅ RetroFit FixIntegrate a scheduling widget (Calendly, Acuity, or Housecall Pro) directly into the website for 24/7 self-service non-emergency appointment booking.
